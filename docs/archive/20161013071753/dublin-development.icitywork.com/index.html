Project Description

The Kaiser Dublin Medical Center project is comprised of 950,000 square feet of medical campus uses in three main buildings, 250,000 square feet of commercial uses, a parking structure, and associated site, roadway frontage, and landscape improvements. Requested land use approvals include a General Plan and Eastern Dublin Specific Plan Amendment to create new land use designations, Planned Development Zoning with related Stage 1 Development Plan for the overall site along with a Stage 2 Development Plan and Site Development Review Permit for the first phase of development – a 220,000 square foot medical office building referred to as Phase 1a.  Certification of a Final Environmental Impact Report (FEIR) and approval of a project-specific Development Agreement is also being considered.

On April 19, 2016, the City Council received a report on the status of the project application and the Planning Commission received a similar report at their meeting on May 10, 2016. The City Council and the Planning Commission provided feedback to Staff and the applicant which was used to finalize the application.

Current Status:

The Planning Commission reviewed the project application on August 23, 2016, and recommended approval to the City Council. The City Council is scheduled to consider the requested entitlements at their meeting on September 20, 2016.

Project Description

Lennar Homes is developing 437 units in 6 neighborhoods, which include 330 detached homes and 107 attached homes.

Neighborhoods 1, 2, 3, 4, and 5 include 330 detached units on small and mid-size lots within the 38 acres designated as Medium Density Residential (6.1 to 14 units per acre).  Neighborhood 6 includes 107 attached condominium townhomes within 7.5 acres designated Medium-High Density Residential (14.1 to 25.0 units per acre) along Lockhart Street.  The remainder of the site includes areas designated as Neighborhood Park, Stream Corridor and Rural Residential/Agriculture  areas.

Medium Density Residential (MDR) – The unit configurations for the Medium-Density Residential areas include Green Court and Auto Court homes.

  • Green Court homes have a front façade oriented towards a common landscaped Green Court or paseo with garage access taken from a private open-ended or terminal alley/drive aisle with as many as eight homes sharing garage access.
  • Auto court homes take auto access from a court area providing access to a maximum of six garages.

Medium-High Density Residential (MHDR) – The Medium-High Density neighborhood is made up of attached townhomes configured as 3-story “6-pack” buildings with front doors facing onto landscaped common-area paseos and garage access from common area alleys.

There are three different architectural styles, which include Contemporary Craftsman, Modern Bungalow and New Prairie.

Current Status:

The project is currently under construction.

Project Description

Dublin Ranch North (formerly known as Redgewick) is 157.7 acres located within the Eastern Dublin Specific Plan Area and originally outside of the City limits. The proposed Project included a request for annexation of the property into the City, General Plan and Eastern Dublin Specific Plan amendments to modify the existing land use designations from Low Density Residential and Rural Residential/Agriculture to 30-acres designated as Estate Residential and 127.3-acres designated as Open Space. The project also included Planned Development Zoning with a related Stage 1 and Stage 2 Development Plan that established development standards and allows construction of four custom estate residential homes (in the future). The area designated as Open Space will be maintained in a permanent conservation easement. The application also included a Development Agreement as required (at the time) by the Eastern Dublin Specific Plan.

Current Status:

The project was approved in December 2009 and the land has since been annexed into the City. Rough grading of the site has been completed. A Site Development Review Permit is required prior to the construction of the four custom homes.

Status

Project Description

The City received a preliminary application from IKEA to construct a store on a 27.5 acre site at 5144 and 5344 Martinelli Way in March 2016.  The site plan is under review and is still being refined by the Applicant (IKEA).  Changes to the sizes of the proposed buildings and the site layout are expected, and the site plan will be refined by the applicant in the near future.  The preliminary plan submitted to the City can be reviewed by clicking on the thumbnail below.  At this point, the site is proposed to be developed with a nearly 378,000 square feet IKEA store, additional retail/restaurant spaces, and possibly a 75-room hotel.  An environmental analysis of the proposed project has begun.

Community Outreach

Over the course of the next several months, the City will provide multiple opportunities and venues for the community to learn about the project and provide input to City Staff on the desires of the community. The City will also provide opportunities for online engagement.

Open House – June 13, 2016

The City’s first community meeting/open house about this project took place on Monday, June 13, 2016 at 6:30 pm at the Dublin Civic Center. The meeting was attended by 45 community members who provided their input and thoughts regarding the project proposal. The informational handouts and feedback form from this meeting are available to review. The feedback form with your comments about the project can be emailed to: kristi.bascom@dublin.ca.gov.

Future Community Meetings – To be scheduled

The City anticipates hosting additional community meetings to discuss topics such as traffic, site planning, architecture and aesthetics of the project. Please contact Kristi Bascom to sign up to receive notice of future community meetings regarding this project. Notices about future meetings on the project will also be posted on this webpage.
