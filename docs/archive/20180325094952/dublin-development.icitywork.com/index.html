Project Description

At Dublin project consists of a mix of lifestyle retail and residential uses on the vacant 76.1 acre parcel generally bound by Tassajara Road, Gleason Drive, Brannigan Street and I-580. The preliminary proposal would support up to 454,500 square feet of commercial uses and up to 680 residential units.

On October 3, 2017, City Council approved the initiation of a General Plan and Eastern Dublin Specific Plan Amendment Study to evaluate changing the land use designation of the subject site.

On October 13, 2017, the City received an application and is undergoing City staff review. As part of the City’s review of the project, staff will determine the associated impacts from the land use change; complete a fiscal impact analysis of the proposed land use changes; conduct the appropriate level of environmental review and documentation; perform any additional studies that may be required; and prepare an analysis of the project for consideration by the Planning Commission and the City Council.

Environmental Review

An environmental analysis of the proposed project has begun. The City released the Notice of Preparation for the Environmental Impact Report (EIR) on January 19, 2018. The 30-day scoping period for the EIR ended on Monday, February 19, 2018. A public scoping meeting was held on Tuesday, January 30, 2018.

Status

Project Description

Safari Kid is proposing a new daycare facility and community room on the vacant 2.1-acre Semi-Public parcel at the corner of Positano Parkway and West Cantara Drive in Positano. The proposed project includes a 14,936 square foot building comprised of a 10,667 square foot childcare center and a 4,269 square foot multipurpose room that will be open for rent to the community.  The project includes an outdoor play area for the childcare center, shared parking for both uses and landscaping throughout the site.

The childcare center is proposed to operate Monday – Friday from 6:30am – 6:30pm with staggered pick-up and drop-off times.  Classrooms and the outdoor play area will not be accessible after the childcare center closes.  The community center is proposed to operate M-F in the evenings and on weekends during the following hours:

-Sunday:  8am-8pm

-Monday-Thursday:  6:30pm-8pm

-Friday:  6:30pm-10pm

-Saturday:  8am-10pm

The application includes a Conditional Use Permit to amend the PD Zoning and a Site Development Review for the Safari Kids Childcare & Community Center.  The project also includes a Minor Use Permit to allow the two uses to utilize shared parking.

On February 13, 2018, the Planning Commission held a public hearing and continued the project.  The project went back to the Planning Commission on April 10, 2018, and it was denied.

The Applicant has appealed the denial and the project is scheduled to go to the City Council on June 19, 2018.

Project Description

The Zeiss Innovation Center includes two phases of research & development (R&D) campus development.

Requested land use approvals include a Planned Development Zoning with a related Stage 1 and Stage 2 Development Plan for the overall site with a Site Development Review for the first phase of development.

Phase 1 will consist of a 3-story R&D building of 208,650 square feet with an entry plaza, a surface parking lot with 663 parking spaces and landscaping throughout the site. Phase 2 will consist of an additional 5-story R&D building of 224,440 square feet and a 5-story, 1,229-space parking garage. A subsequent Site Development Review will be required for Phase 2.

At build-out, the proposed project would include two low-to-mid rise (three-story and five-story) R&D buildings totaling 433,090 square feet and would be used for research, development and testing, light assembly and dry laboratories, and supporting office spaces. Other internal uses would include conference rooms, an employee cafeteria, and a demonstration center/showroom. At build-out parking would include one parking garage with 1,229 spaces and 167 surface  parking spaces, for a total of 1,396 spaces.

The site would accommodate approximately 1,500 employees at full build-out.

The project was approved by the City Council on March 20, 2018.

Medium Density Residential (MDR) – The unit configurations for the Medium-Density Residential areas include Green Court and Auto Court homes.

  • Green Court homes have a front façade oriented towards a common landscaped Green Court or paseo with garage access taken from a private open-ended or terminal alley/drive aisle with as many as eight homes sharing garage access.
  • Auto court homes take auto access from a court area providing access to a maximum of six garages.

Medium-High Density Residential (MHDR) – The Medium-High Density neighborhood is made up of attached townhomes configured as 3-storis and typically include 6 units per building with front doors facing onto landscaped common-area paseos, and garage access from common area alleys.

There are three different architectural styles, which include Contemporary Craftsman, Modern Bungalow and New Prairie.

Current Status:

Project Description

The City received an application from IKEA to construct a store and a commercial center on a 27.5 acre site at 5144 and 5344 Martinelli Way.  The proposed project is for a 339,099 square foot IKEA store with integrated podium parking and an approximately 93,000 square foot commercial center with a mix of retail and restaurant spaces.  The updated site plan submitted to the City can be reviewed by clicking on the thumbnail below.

Environmental Review

The City released the Draft Supplemental Environmental Impact Report (EIR) on January 31, 2018. The public comment period on the EIR ended on March 16, 2018.

Community Outreach

As part of the City’s review of the project, the City provided multiple opportunities and venues for the community to learn about the project and provide input to City Staff on the desires of the community. Details below:

Community Meeting  “Site Design and Aesthetics” – February 21, 2018

The City hosted its third community meeting for the IKEA Retail Center Project on the topic of “Site Design and Aesthetics” on Wednesday, February 21, 2018, at 6:00 p.m. City staff was joined by the Applicant’s design professionals including the architects, landscape architects, and civil engineer to provide a short presentation on the proposed site design and architecture for the IKEA Retail Center.  

Community Meeting “Transportation and Traffic” – December 6, 2017

The City of Dublin hosted its second Community Meeting to engage the residents of Dublin on the IKEA Retail Center development proposal. This meeting focused on the topics of Transportation and Traffic. 

Open House – June 13, 2016

The City’s first community meeting/open house about this project took place on Monday, June 13, 2016 at 6:30 pm at the Dublin Civic Center. The meeting was attended by 45 community members who provided their input and thoughts regarding the project proposal. The informational handouts and feedback form from this meeting are available to review.
