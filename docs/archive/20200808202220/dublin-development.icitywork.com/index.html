Planning Commission April 28, 2020 Agenda & Staff Report

Status

Project Description

Site Development Review Permit to construct a new 138 room hotel in the parking lot south of the existing office building (Corrie Center). The proposed 4-story hotel is located above a 2-story parking structure to be shared with the existing office building. The applications include a minor use permit for shared parking, Heritage Tree Removal Permit  to remove two Coast redwoods, community benefit agreement and related improvements to landscaping and parking.

Background

Planning Commission approved the project on April 28, 2020. As part of the Planning Commission’s approval of the Heritage Tree Removal Permit, the Planning Commission placed a condition that required the Applicant to work with Staff to find possible solutions that would protect the two heritage redwood trees provided that no changed to the hotel footprint would be required.

The Applicant has explored a solution to shift the bioretention area into the adjacent parking stalls so that the trees may remain in place. As a result, the Applicant has withdrawn the Heritage Tree Removal Application and Permit (PLOC-2020-00053) to remove the two Coast redwood trees. Therefore, the Applicant is no longer authorized to remove the two trees.

On May 7, 2020, an appeal of the Planning’s Commission decision to approve  the Site Development Review Permit and Minor Use Permit was filed.

Current Status

The City Council approved the Site Development Review Permit, Minor Use Permit and Community Benefit Program Agreement on June 2, 2020.

Ashton @ Dublin Station July 17, 2018 City Council Staff Report

Status

Project Description

The proposed Ashton at Dublin Station project is located at the northwest corner of DeMarcus Boulevard and Campbell Lane within the Transit Center. The site has a General Plan and Eastern Dublin Specific Plan land use designation of High Density Residential (25+ units per acre) and Planned Development Zoning with a Stage 1 Development Plan that permits a high density residential development.

The Applicant proposes to construct a 220-unit apartment community and related amenities which include a fitness center, pool, roof top lounge, and 331 structured parking spaces. The proposed project includes 3, 4 and 5 story elements that are over 2 levels of parking, and vary in height from 61 feet to 83 feet at the peak of the tallest roof element.

On November 14, 2017, the Planning Commission unanimously recommended approval of the proposed project. The City Council held public meetings to consider the proposed project on December 5, 2017, February 6, 2018 and March 6, 2018. Ultimately, the City Council denied the project on March 6, 2018. The Applicant subsequently filed a lawsuit against the City. The City and the Applicant have entered into a settlement agreement. In accordance with the terms of the settlement agreement, the City Council reconsidered the project, and considered entering into a Development Agreement. Please refer to the link under “Documents” (above) for more information about the settlement agreement.

The City Council approved the project on July 17, 2018. The project is  currently in plan check.

Status

Project Description

Background

The At Dublin project consists of a mix of lifestyle retail and residential uses on the vacant 76.1 acre parcel generally bound by Tassajara Road, Gleason Drive, Brannigan Street and I-580. The preliminary proposal included up to 400,500 square feet of commercial uses and up to 665 residential units.

On October 3, 2017, City Council approved the initiation of a General Plan and Eastern Dublin Specific Plan Amendment Study to evaluate changing the land use designation of the subject site.

On October 13, 2017, the City received an application. As part of the City’s review of the project, staff analyzed the impacts from the land use change; completed a fiscal impact analysis of the proposed land use changes; conducted environmental review and prepared an environmental impact report; and prepared an analysis of the project for consideration by the Planning Commission and the City Council.

On October 30, 2018, the Planning Commission held a Public Hearing to review the proposed project and made a recommendation to City Council.  On November 20, 2018, following a request from the Applicant, the City Council continued the project to an undetermined future date.

Since the November 20, 2018 meeting, the Applicant has evaluated potential revisions to the project. The City Council held a Study Session on June 4, 2019 and received a presentation from the Applicant regarding the potential project revisions. No action was taken or approvals granted for the project at this meeting. Please see the link on the right side of the page for this conceptual plan that was presented to the City Council.

In October 2019, the Applicant submitted revised project materials in response to public comments received.  The revised project materials which include a reduction to 566 residential units and 240,000 square feet of commercial uses is under review.

On February 25, 2020, the Planning Commission recommended denial of the General Plan and Eastern Dublin Specific Plan and the At Dublin project to the City Council.

Current Status

On June 22, 2020, City Council took final action and denied the project.

Environmental Review

The Draft Environmental Impact Report (DEIR) was released on July 6, 2018 for a 45-public review period, which ended on August 20, 2018.  The Final EIR, which includes responses to comments received on the Draft EIR, was released on October 24, 2018. Based on the revised project submitted in October 2019, the City prepared an Errata to the EIR.  Pursuant to CEQA Guidelines Section 15088.5, the City analyzed the revised project and determined that there was no new significant information or new significant environmental impact as a result of the revised project.  The DEIR, Final EIR and Errata are available to view on this webpage under “Documents”.

Project Description

The Zeiss Innovation Center includes two phases of research & development (R&D) campus development.

Requested land use approvals include a Planned Development Zoning with a related Stage 1 and Stage 2 Development Plan for the overall site with a Site Development Review for the first phase of development.

Phase 1 will consist of a 3-story R&D building of 208,650 square feet with an entry plaza, a surface parking lot with 663 parking spaces and landscaping throughout the site. Phase 2 will consist of an additional 5-story R&D building of 224,440 square feet and a 5-story, 1,229-space parking garage. A subsequent Site Development Review will be required for Phase 2.

At build-out, the proposed project would include two low-to-mid rise (three-story and five-story) R&D buildings totaling 433,090 square feet and would be used for research, development and testing, light assembly and dry laboratories, and supporting office spaces. Other internal uses would include conference rooms, an employee cafeteria, and a demonstration center/showroom. At build-out parking would include one parking garage with 1,229 spaces and 167 surface  parking spaces, for a total of 1,396 spaces.

The site would accommodate approximately 1,500 employees at full build-out.

The project was approved by the City Council on March 20, 2018 and building permits have been issued for the first phase of construction.
